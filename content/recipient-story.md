# Our Story — Rochelle & Cody Thomas
*Spring 2026 BabyQuest Grant Recipients*

---

We found out on the evening of April 6, 2026, that we had been selected as Spring 2026
BabyQuest grant recipients. We sat with that news for a long time before we knew what to
say about it. What we keep coming back to is this: there are people out there who built
this foundation so that couples like us would not have to choose between starting a family
and financial security. We want more people to know that.

This is our story.

---

Over the past twelve years, Rochelle has helped raise several children as a nanny. She has
celebrated their first steps, comforted them through scraped knees, and watched them grow
into curious and kind young people. Few things bring her more joy than guiding children
through those early years. Yet each time she leaves for the day, she carries the same quiet
hope that one day she and Cody will experience those moments with a child of their own.

We are Rochelle and Cody, a couple grounded in love, purpose, and a shared vision for the
family we hope to build. While our journey to parenthood has not unfolded the way we
expected, our story is not defined by hardship. Instead, it is shaped by the values we
carry from our upbringings: family, compassion, hard work, and showing up for the people
you love. Like many couples facing infertility, we have experienced uncertainty and
heartbreak, but those experiences have strengthened our commitment to building a family
together.

Family has always been central to Rochelle's life. As the youngest of five children, she
grew up surrounded by siblings who looked out for one another and shared both
responsibilities and laughter. Those experiences shaped Rochelle into the nurturing and
compassionate person she is today and instilled in her a deep appreciation for the role
family plays in shaping who we become.

That love for family naturally guided Rochelle to a career as a nanny. For over a decade
she has dedicated herself to caring for children and supporting the families who raise
them. Rochelle has become part of three families, forming bonds well beyond employment.
The children she cares for know her not simply as a caregiver, but as someone who
genuinely loves and supports them. Through her work she has developed a deep understanding
of childhood development and the importance of consistency, encouragement, and patience.
Helping guide children through their early years has brought Rochelle tremendous
fulfillment and deepened her dream of becoming a mother. Watching her care for children
with such devotion has also strengthened Cody's desire to become a father and the life
they hope to build together.

Cody grew up in a home where commitment and family were lived examples. His parents have
been married for more than forty years, providing a steady model of partnership, respect,
and shared responsibility. As the oldest of two children, he learned early what it meant
to look out for someone else. Those experiences shaped the thoughtful perspective Cody
brings to life and reinforced his belief that family is something you care for
intentionally.

Outside of work, we find joy in simple things. Rochelle enjoys volleyball, reading, and
staying active, while Cody enjoys golf and reading essays that challenge his thinking and
perspective. Friends and family often describe Rochelle as kind, genuine, and deeply
caring, while Cody is known for being thoughtful and analytical. Rochelle brings warmth
and spontaneity to our lives, while Cody brings focus and stability. Together these
differences strengthen our relationship and help us approach life as a team.

We met through mutual acquaintances, and what quickly brought us together was our shared
commitment to honest communication and our desire to build a meaningful life. Our
relationship is built on trust, laughter, and deep respect for one another's strengths. We
envision a family life centered around togetherness: time spent around the dinner table,
supporting one another through challenges, and celebrating the everyday moments that make
life meaningful.

To us, family also means community. Whether it is babysitting, attending a niece or
nephew's sporting event, helping friends host a kid's party, or driving hours for a short
family gathering, we believe in showing up for the people we love. If we are fortunate
enough to become parents, our child will grow up surrounded by people who love them deeply
and who are eager to support us along the way.

We believe we are well suited for parenthood because of the lives we have built and the
experiences we bring. Rochelle's years nurturing children have prepared her for the daily
responsibilities of parenting, while Cody's steady and thoughtful approach to family
reflects the values he hopes to pass on to the next generation.

We are not perfect people, but we are intentional people. We communicate openly, make
decisions with care, and approach life with gratitude and purpose.

---

We write this now not because we were asked to, but because we know there are other
Rochelles and Codys out there — people who have been sitting with the same quiet hope,
running the same impossible math, and wondering whether this dream is still within reach.

BabyQuest exists because of donors who decided that it should be. If our story moves you,
the most meaningful thing you can do is give — even a small recurring gift helps fund the
next grant cycle and brings another family one step closer.
